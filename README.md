Smart City Complaint Analysis
📌 Project Overview

The Smart City Complaint Analysis project focuses on analyzing citizen complaints related to public services such as traffic congestion, street light failures, drainage issues, water supply, public safety, and infrastructure maintenance. The objective is to transform raw complaint data into actionable insights using Python and Power BI, helping city authorities improve service delivery, operational efficiency, and citizen satisfaction.

🎯 Problem Statement

Smart cities receive thousands of complaints daily. Manual monitoring of complaint trends and service performance is difficult and can lead to delayed resolutions and reduced citizen satisfaction.

This project aims to:

Identify complaint patterns and trends
Detect complaint hotspot zones
Evaluate department performance
Analyze complaint resolution efficiency
Improve citizen satisfaction through data-driven insights
🎯 Objectives
Analyze complaint patterns across city zones
Identify the most frequent complaint categories
Evaluate complaint resolution performance
Investigate the relationship between complaint priority and resolution delay
Detect complaint hotspot areas
Assess department efficiency and citizen satisfaction
Provide recommendations for improving city services

🔍 Key Business Insights
1. Most Frequent Complaint Categories
Traffic Congestion generated the highest number of complaints.
Street Light Failures and Drainage Problems were also frequently reported.
Water Supply and Public Safety complaints contributed significantly to overall complaint volume.
2. Complaint Hotspot Areas
South Zone recorded the highest complaint volume.
Indicates recurring infrastructure and civic service issues.
Requires prioritized maintenance and resource allocation.
3. Departments with Longest Resolution Time
Electrical Department
Traffic Control Department
Public Safety Department

These departments required approximately 10 days on average to resolve complaints.
